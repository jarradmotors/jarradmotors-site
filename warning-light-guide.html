Warning Light Guide

A warning light does not always mean panic, but it should not be ignored. This guide helps you decide whether to stop safely, call for help, or book the vehicle in for testing.

Quick rule

Red warning light: stop when safe and call for advice. Amber warning light: book the vehicle in soon. Flashing engine light: avoid driving if possible, as continued driving can cause damage.

Common warning lights

Check engine light

Can relate to emissions, sensors, misfires, fuel trims, boost faults or many other systems. We scan the vehicle, test the cause and explain the repair options.

Flashing engine light

This can indicate a misfire that may damage the catalytic converter. Reduce load, avoid long driving and call the workshop.

Battery or charging light

The vehicle may be running from battery power only. It could stop once the battery drains. This needs prompt testing.

Oil pressure light

Stop safely and switch the engine off. Low oil pressure can cause serious engine damage quickly.

Temperature warning

Pull over safely, switch off and let the engine cool. Do not remove a hot radiator cap.

ABS, traction or stability lights

The vehicle may still brake, but safety systems may be disabled. Book it in for diagnosis.

Airbag or SRS light

The airbag system may not operate correctly in a crash. We can test SRS faults and related wiring issues.

DPF or diesel warning lights

Diesel warning lights can relate to soot loading, sensors, EGR, boost or regeneration problems. Early testing can avoid expensive damage.

Before you call or book

If it is safe, note the vehicle make and model, the exact warning message, whether the light is red, amber or flashing, and what the vehicle was doing when it appeared. Photos of the dash warning can help.
