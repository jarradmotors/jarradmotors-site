Dash warning lights

Common warning lights and what to do

Some warning lights mean “book it in soon”. Others mean “stop driving now”. This guide gives simple, practical advice for drivers around Casterton and surrounding areas.

Book a Check Call the Workshop
Important: these are general guidelines only. If the vehicle feels unsafe, smells hot, loses oil or coolant, loses braking assistance, or starts running badly, pull over safely and call for help. RACV members can call 13 11 11. For non-RACV after-hours breakdowns only, call 0491 224 717.

Red warning lights

Red warning lights usually need immediate attention. Do not ignore them or keep driving just to “see how it goes”.

Oil pressure light

Stop now

Low oil pressure can destroy an engine very quickly. Pull over safely, switch the engine off and do not keep driving.

RACV members: call 13 11 11 Non-RACV after-hours breakdown: 0491 224 717

Temperature / overheating light

Stop and let it cool

Pull over safely and switch the engine off. Do not remove a hot radiator cap. Driving overheated can turn a simple cooling fault into major engine damage.

RACV members: call 13 11 11 Non-RACV after-hours breakdown: 0491 224 717

Brake warning light

Do not risk it

If the brake warning light is red, the pedal feels wrong, or the vehicle is losing brake fluid, do not continue driving. Brakes need to be treated seriously.

RACV members: call 13 11 11 During business hours: call the workshop

Amber warning lights

Amber lights usually mean the vehicle needs checking. Some can still become urgent depending on how the vehicle feels and behaves.

Engine light

Book a scan

If the engine light is on but the vehicle drives normally, book it in for a diagnostic scan and testing. It may be emissions, sensor, misfire or engine management related.

Book an engine light check Read more about engine lights

Flashing engine light

Call first

A flashing engine light often means a misfire that can damage the catalytic converter or engine. Avoid driving far and call the workshop for advice.

Call the workshop Book online if safe to drive

Battery / charging light

Check soon

This can mean the alternator is not charging. The vehicle may keep driving briefly, then stop once battery voltage drops.

ABS light

Book testing

Normal braking may still work, but anti-lock braking may be disabled. Book it in so the ABS system can be scanned and tested.

Book ABS diagnosis Call the workshop

Airbag / SRS light

Safety system fault

The airbag or seatbelt pretensioner system may not operate correctly in a crash. Book it in for proper scan and circuit testing.

Book SRS diagnosis Auto electrical repairs

DPF light

Diesel warning

A DPF warning can mean the diesel particulate filter needs regeneration or has a fault. Do not ignore it until it blocks completely.

Book DPF check Call for advice

Tyre pressure light

Check tyres

Check tyre pressures and look for a puncture. If the tyre is going flat, do not keep driving on it.

Book tyre/pressure check Call the workshop

Key / immobiliser light

Key issue

If the car will not recognise the key, will not crank, or starts then cuts out, it may be key, immobiliser or programming related.

Book service

A service reminder usually means the vehicle is due for scheduled maintenance. Keeping up servicing helps prevent bigger repair bills later.

Quick answers

A warning light does not always mean a huge repair, but it does mean the vehicle is asking for attention.

Can I drive with the engine light on?

If it is steady and the car drives normally, usually book it in soon. If it is flashing, running rough, lacking power, overheating or making unusual noises, stop and call for advice.

Do you work on common makes?

Yes. We see common local vehicles including Toyota Corolla, Camry and Hilux, Ford Ranger, Mazda 3, Mazda CX-5, Hyundai i30, Mitsubishi Triton, Nissan Navara, Isuzu D-MAX, Holden Commodore and Holden Colorado.

Do you just scan the vehicle?

A scan is the starting point, not the whole diagnosis. We use scan data, wiring information, test equipment and hands-on checks to work out what is actually wrong.

What should I tell the workshop?

Tell us which light is on, when it appeared, whether the car drives differently, and whether anything else happened at the same time. Photos of the dash can help too.
